Work Experience

During my two-month internship at Majlis Daerah Tangkak in the ICT Department, I learned a lot about working in an office and how technology is used in an organization. Although I had never worked before, this internship gave me the chance to try different tasks and develop new skills. I helped with both technical and multimedia jobs, which made the experience fun and interesting.

One of my first tasks was to help prepare equipment for meetings, such as setting up projectors and laptops. This seemed like a simple job, but it taught me the importance of being organized and making sure everything worked properly before meetings. Another important task I had was renewing the antivirus licenses for employees' desktops. This was essential to keep the office computers safe and secure, and I learned how to manage software updates and ensure that everything was up-to-date.

I also worked on several multimedia projects, like making banners and posters for events. For example, I created designs for Malaysia’s Day and created a poster for celebrating KDYMM Seri Paduka Baginda Raja Permaisuri Agong Raja Zarith Sofia's birthday. I also helped make a "Garis Panduan" (Guideline Book) for the ICT Department to explain how things should be done. These tasks helped me improve my design skills and learn how to make materials that look professional.

One of the most meaningful tasks I did was making a video montage for retired employees. I collected photos and videos of their time working at Majlis Daerah Tangkak and put them together to create a tribute. This project taught me how to edit videos and tell a story with pictures and music. Overall, my internship helped me learn new skills and gave me a better understanding of how an office works. It was a great experience, and I’m excited to use what I learned in my future career.
